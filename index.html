My Internship Experience as a Web Developer

Stepping into my Web Development Internship has been one of the most exciting and transforming phases of my learning journey. It’s the first time I moved from academic projects to working on real-time development tasks, and the shift taught me more than I ever imagined.During this internship, I had the opportunity to work on a salon management website where I helped build both the user-facing interface and the admin panel. This gave me hands-on exposure to real workflows — from designing responsive layouts and validating forms to writing backend logic and managing data flow between client and server. One of the biggest takeaways for me was understanding the importance of clean code, collaboration, and version control in a real-world setting. I learned how to communicate with teammates, fix bugs, meet deadlines, and adapt to feedback — all while constantly leveling up my technical skills. This experience also helped me build confidence in using tools like Git, working with APIs, and handling both frontend and backend responsibilities. More than just code, it taught me how to think like a developer — to problem-solve, optimize, and deliver with purpose. My internship wasn’t just about learning to build websites — it was about becoming a better, faster, more thoughtful web developer. And this is just the beginning.

UI/UX with Figma

Exploring UI/UX with Figma

As someone stepping into the world of web development, I realized that building beautiful and intuitive interfaces is just as important as writing clean code. That’s when I started exploring Figma — and it quickly became one of my favorite tools. In the beginning, Figma felt like a digital sketchpad — but with every click, I discovered how powerful and professional it really is. From wireframing layouts to creating full-fledged UI designs, Figma helped me visualize ideas before turning them into code. I started with basic layouts and components, and then moved on to designing complete pages — login screens, dashboards, user flows, and more. I learned about grids, spacing, typography, and color theory — and how all of these details come together to shape the user experience. What I love most about Figma is how collaborative it is. Even as a beginner, I could work in real-time with mentors or follow along with community files and tutorials. It made UI/UX design feel accessible and fun — not intimidating. Learning Figma has definitely sharpened my front-end skills. Now when I build websites, I don’t just think in code — I think in structure, emotion, and flow. It’s a creative side of tech I’m excited to keep exploring
